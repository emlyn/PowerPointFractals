PowerPoint Fractal Art

All of these images were created by Emlyn Corrin using only Microsoft PowerPoint.

You can download the PowerPoint files to see how they were made, or to modify them and create your own fractal images, by clicking the icon in the description of each fractal. You are free to use or modify them as you like for your own personal use, as long as you credit the author and make any derivative works available under the same license (they are licensed under a Creative Commons BY-NC-SA license). If you would like to use them for commercial purposes, please contact the author.

Triangles

Fractals with triangular symmetry, such as the Sierpiński Triangle and variations.

Squares

Fractals with square symmetry, such as the Sierpiński Carpet and variations.

Pentagons

Fractals with pentagonal symmetry.

Hexagons

Fractals with hexagonal symmetry, such as the Koch Snowflake.

Linear

Fractals where all repeats appear on a straight line, with no rotation ("fractalness" is one-dimensional).

Curves

Fractal curves (such as the Koch Curve and the Dragon Curve).

Plants

Fractal plants (such as leaves and trees).

Nature

Other things from nature that are not plants.

Spirals

Spiral fractals.

Abstract

Abstract shapes that don't fit into any of the other categories.

3D

Three dimensional fractals
